Solving probabilistic planning problems over-constrained by limited risk

Definitions:

A planning problem is over-constrained when it is not possible to reach all goals because of limited resources (e.g. fuel, energy...). In this case, only a subset of goals can be satisfied. Therefore, each goal is attached a utility score and the objective if to find a plan that satisfy a subset of goals maximizing the total goal utilities. This category of problems have been studied under the name of Over-Subscription Planning problems (OSPs). Let's take an example: we consider that a rover on Mars must reach 4 locations (to explore and collect samples) but it has limited in energy that prevent it from reaching all the locations. In this case, we can say that the problem is over-constrained. We also suppose that each location has a utiliy value corresponding to the level of interest of the location. Therefore, we must select locations that are reachable and maximize the total utility score.

In this example, the rover has not enough energy to reach L2. It has selected L1, L3 and L4 as it maximizes the total utility. These kinf of problems deal with determinstic planning problems that are over-constrained by limited ressources. In my work, I got interested in probabilistic planning where problems are over-constrained by limited risk the decision-maker is ready to take.

In the context of probabilistic planning, we defined the risk as the probability to not reach all goals and the reachibility probability (RP) as the probability to reach all goals. We define a probability threshold named the safe threshold alpha We say that a solution is safe if its RP is above alpha and that a problem is over-constrained if no safe solution exist. Let's go back to our example. We suppose that the rover takes some risk every time he moves, for example because of bump and sand that could block it. In the below picture, there is no safe solution as reaching all 4 locations is too risky (with regard to the treshold alpha). Therefore, we will reduce the number of targetted locations until we can propose a safe solution. In our example, the rover will only target L1, L2 and L4. The idea is to consider that we prefer reaching less locations instead of taking the risk thay the rover remains blocked and get lost.
